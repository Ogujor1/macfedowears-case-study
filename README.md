# Macfedowears Case Study

A self-funded direct-to-consumer footwear brand based in Lagos, specializing in handcrafted halfshoes, sandals, and slippers inspired by the Birkenstock silhouette but unaffiliated with any external label. This repository documents the WordPress e-commerce build phase of the brand, from concept through to launch.

## Live Site

[www.macfedowears.com](https://www.macfedowears.com)

The active storefront has since been migrated to a funnel-based platform (Systeme.io) for current sales and marketing operations. This case study covers the prior WordPress / WooCommerce build.

## Brand Overview

**Category:** Footwear (men's and women's)
**Product lines:** Halfshoes, Halfshoe Sandals, Sandals, Slippers
**Positioning:** Modern, durable, affordable
**Region served:** Nigeria, with international shipping available

## Why This Build Existed

I started Macfedowears on Shopify. As the brand grew, I wanted full control over the storefront design, payment flow, and customer experience without the constraints of a SaaS platform. In early 2026, I rebuilt the entire store on WordPress with WooCommerce to give the brand a more professional, owned presence.

The migration to Systeme.io came later, as the brand's focus shifted toward funnel-based marketing and direct response selling.

## What Was Built

### Storefront

- **Custom-styled Blocksy theme** matched to the brand's visual identity (warm, earthy palette, generous whitespace, editorial product layouts).
- **Four product categories** with dedicated landing pages: Halfshoes, Halfshoe Sandals, Sandals, Slippers.
- **Product detail pages** with multiple images, variant selection (size, color), inventory tracking.

### Checkout

- **Custom checkout flow** built to minimize friction for Nigerian customers.
- Reduced default WooCommerce fields to essentials only.
- Trust signals throughout (free shipping threshold, return policy, warranty).

### Payments

- **Paystack** integration (card, bank transfer, USSD).
- **Flutterwave** integration (card, mobile money, international cards).
- Test mode and live mode flows verified.

### Customer trust

- Free shipping on orders over ₦200,000.
- 30-day return window.
- 6-month product warranty.
- Clear shipping and refund policies.

## What I Learned

Three things I took away from this build that have shaped how I approach e-commerce projects since:

1. **Owned platforms cost more upfront, but they pay back in control.** Migrating off Shopify was painful, but every checkout customization, every payment integration, every design tweak became possible.

2. **Customer trust signals matter more than design polish.** A clear refund policy, a visible warranty, and a known payment provider (Paystack) converted better than any homepage redesign.

3. **Platforms are tools, not destinations.** The eventual migration to Systeme.io taught me that the best platform is the one that serves the current stage of the business. WordPress was right for the catalog-and-credibility phase. A funnel builder is right for the conversion-and-scale phase.
